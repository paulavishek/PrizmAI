What is AI Retrospectives?

AI-facilitated retrospective sessions that structure team learning into four quadrants, then automatically synthesize patterns across multiple retros to surface recurring themes. Insights become tracked action items assigned to real people — so lessons actually get implemented, not just discussed.

The four quadrants of a retrospective
What Worked

Team practices, processes, or decisions that should be repeated. Reinforces positive patterns.

What Didn’t Work

Friction, failures, or blockers that slowed the team. These become the source of improvement actions.

Ideas

Experiments to try next sprint. Low-friction suggestions that could be tested quickly.

Action Items

Specific, assigned, time-bound improvements. These are tracked to completion on this dashboard.

AI Synthesis

After the team fills in the quadrants, the AI reads all cards across every past retro and identifies recurring themes — patterns that keep appearing sprint after sprint. These surface as synthesized insights you can convert to action items.

Recurring Issue — the same theme appeared in 2 or more retros. The AI flags these as systemic problems, not one-offs.

Action Tracking & Metrics

Every insight that matters becomes a tracked action item — assigned to a person, with a due date and status. The dashboard shows three key metrics:

Implementation Rate % of insights converted to action items
Completion Rate % of action items actually completed
Recurring Issues Problems that appear in 2+ retros
Workflow
  1. Create a retro session — team adds cards to each quadrant
  2. AI synthesizes patterns across all past retros
  3. Convert key insights into action items with owners
  4. Track implementation until completion rate reaches your target
