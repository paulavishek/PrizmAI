What is Scope Autopsy?

A forensic post-mortem that traces every scope expansion in your project to its exact cause, contributor, date, and cost. Like a detective reconstructing a crime scene, the Scope Autopsy builds a timeline of how your project grew beyond its original boundaries — then generates exportable PDF reports that turn scope history into actionable lessons for future projects.

Four forensic dimensions analysed for every scope change
When

The exact date each scope change was introduced, building a chronological timeline of drift.

Who

Which team member introduced or approved the change, enabling honest accountability.

Why

The root cause category: requirement change, stakeholder request, discovered complexity, or gold plating.

Cost

The quantified impact: days delayed or budget overrun attributable to this specific change.

Scope change categories
Category Typical frequency Preventable? Best action
Requirement Change High Partially Better upfront specs
Stakeholder Request Medium Yes — via change control Formal change request process
Discovered Complexity Low–Medium Partially Better Pre-Mortem analysis
Gold Plating Low Yes Strict scope discipline
What you get from an autopsy
  • Forensic timeline — every scope change plotted chronologically
  • AI root cause analysis — the dominant pattern driving your drift
  • Cost attribution — total delay and budget impact quantified
  • Exportable PDF report — share findings with stakeholders
Recommended workflow
  1. Run the Autopsy — AI reconstructs your scope history
  2. Read the forensic timeline — spot the inflection points
  3. Identify the dominant change category driving drift
  4. Act on the root cause pattern for future projects
  5. Export the PDF report for stakeholder review
